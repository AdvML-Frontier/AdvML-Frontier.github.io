About AdvML 2022

About AdvML 2022

Adversarial machine learning (AdvML), which aims at tricking ML models by providing deceptive inputs, has been identified as a powerful method to improve various trustworthiness metrics (e.g., adversarial robustness, explainability, and fairness) and to advance versatile ML paradigms (e.g., supervised and self-supervised learning, and static and continual learning). As a consequence of the proliferation of AdvML-inspired research works, the proposed workshop – New Frontiers in AdvML – aims to identify the challenges and limitations of current AdvML methods, and explore new prospective and constructive views of AdvML across the full theory/algorithm/application stack.

Call For Papers

Submission Instructions

We welcome full paper submissions (up to 6 pages and “blue sky” extended abstract submissions (up to 2 pages), excluding references or supplementary materials). The paper submissions must be in pdf format and use the ICML official templates. All submissions must be anonymous and conform to ICML standards for double-blind review. The accepted papers will be posted on the workshop website and will not appear in the ICML proceedings.

Topics

The topics for AdvML 2022 include, but are not limited to:

Theory perspective:

  • Mathematics foundations of AdvML (e.g., geometries of learning, causality, information theory).
  • AdvML metrics and their interconnections.
  • Neurobiology-inspired AdvML foundations, and others beyond machine-centric design.

Algorithm perspective:

  • New optimization methods for AdvML.
  • Data foundations of AdvML (e.g., new datasets and new data-driven algorithms).
  • Scalable AdvML on the edge.
  • Physical AdvML (e.g., physical attacks and lifelong defenses).
  • Distributed AdvML.

Application perspective:

  • Distributed AdvML.
  • New AdvML applications.
  • AdvML for social good (e.g., education, healthcare, and scientific discovery).
